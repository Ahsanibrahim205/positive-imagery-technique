What is the positive
imagery technique?

  • The Positive Mental Imagery Technique helps improve performance by enabling future events to be experienced in the form of mental images in a positive way prior to them being acted out for real.
  • Situations and adaptive responses are pictured in the mind in as realistic a way as possible. These anticipations are particularly relevant to successful physical performances – that is, whenever you do something involving movement!
  • Coaches and athletes using this technique in sport sometimes refer to it as ‘visualisation’ and use ‘performance scripting’ to help guide the imagery.
  • It contrasts to the Positive Self-Talk techniques in that it focuses on visual images rather than ‘inner self talk’.
  • A useful extension of the Positive Mental Imagery technique is to use it for ‘contingency planning’.
  • Contingency Planning Imagery involves
      picturing in your mind’s eye a performance problem arising
      sensing the experience of it arising
      experiencing working through it to reach a successful outcome.

    • This imagery could relate to a problematic experience that in the past has given rise to excessive anxiety. You picture it arising again and, this time, you being able to successfully carry out a coping behaviour that you have selected.
